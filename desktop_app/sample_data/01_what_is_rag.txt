What Is Retrieval-Augmented Generation?

Retrieval-Augmented Generation, or RAG, is a technique that combines two powerful ideas:
information retrieval and language generation.

Instead of relying solely on what a language model memorised during training, RAG lets the model
look up relevant documents at query time and use them as context when generating an answer.
This means answers are grounded in real, up-to-date information rather than stale training data.

How It Works

1. Your documents are broken into chunks and converted into numerical representations called
   embeddings. Each embedding captures the meaning of a piece of text as a point in a high-
   dimensional space.

2. When a user asks a question, that question is also converted to an embedding.

3. The system finds the document chunks whose embeddings are closest to the question embedding.
   This is the "retrieval" step.

4. Those chunks are passed to a language model as context, and the model generates a response
   that draws on the retrieved material.

Why It Matters

- Accuracy: The model can cite actual documents instead of hallucinating facts.
- Freshness: You can add new documents at any time without retraining.
- Transparency: You can show users exactly which source was used to answer their question.
- Control: Your data stays on your infrastructure — nothing is sent to an external model
  unless you explicitly choose a cloud provider.

PGVectorRAGIndexer gives you the retrieval layer: it stores your document embeddings in
PostgreSQL using the pgvector extension and serves fast similarity-search queries over them.
